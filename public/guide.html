Yangon Tyre Portal — User Guide

ops.supermega.dev · For all roles
← Back to portal

What is this portal?

The Yangon Tyre Operations Portal is a private, phone-friendly dashboard that gives the YTF team a single live view of the factory — production numbers, raw-material stock, warranty claims, orders, receivables, and quality scores — without replacing anything you already use.

Two surfaces — one system

SurfaceURLBest for
Operations cockpit (this guide)ops.supermega.devOwner / managers on phone — live numbers, alerts, captures
YTF ERP portalytf.supermega.devStaff at desk — data entry, daily close, work board, payroll

The cockpit reads from Drive, Gmail, Viber, and Galaxy iStock automatically. The ERP is where staff record and approve data. Neither replaces the other.

Important This portal is private to Yangon Tyre. Your passcode never appears in the URL bar. Do not share the passcode with anyone outside the company.

How to access

  1. Open your browser and go to ops.supermega.dev (or the current stable URL — ask admin if unsure).
  2. Enter your passcode when prompted. Each role has its own passcode — do not mix accounts.
  3. Add to home screen (recommended): in Safari/Chrome, use "Add to Home Screen" so it opens as an app without the browser bar, with full portrait/landscape support.

Install as a mobile app (PWA)

DeviceSteps
iPhone / iPad (Safari)Tap the Share button → Add to Home Screen → Add
Android (Chrome)Tap the three-dot menu → Add to Home screen
Desktop (Chrome)Address bar → install icon on the right
Tip The portal works offline for the last-loaded data. Pull down on the home screen to force a data refresh (or tap the refresh button in admin panel).

Roles & access levels

Every user has one of four roles. Your role controls what you see and what actions you can trigger. The admin console sets who has which role.

Role What they see Gmail data Can run refresh
admin Everything + system health + pipeline controls. Can see all groups, all modules, and the admin console. No Full
ceo Everything including finance, procurement (TFT), warranty claims with email context, and company-wide view. Yes (only role) Full
manager Cross-plant ops — production, stock, quality, orders. Head-office view. No email-sourced private data. No Pipeline + insights
plant_manager Their plant's numbers only — production, stock, quality, and their Viber group. Cannot see cross-plant finance. No Pipeline only
Security Gmail-sourced data (procurement costs, TFT threads, financial claims) is only ever shown to the CEO role. If you are not logged in as CEO, those sections will be empty or hidden — this is correct behavior.

Viber groups per role

Your role also controls which Viber groups you can see in the capture and notice board. A plant_manager for Plant A cannot see Plant B's Viber channel. The admin sets group assignments in PANEL_USERS.

RoleDefault groups
adminAll groups (*)
ceoAll groups (*)
managerplant-a, plant-b, head-office
plant_managerTheir plant only (e.g. plant-a)

System details (collapsed)

The "System status" section is collapsed by default. Tap it to see pipeline health, last refresh time, and job status. This is for admin / tech troubleshooting — normal users do not need to open it.

Full Detail screen (ops.html)

Reached via the Full detail → button. Shows every operational module in one scrolling page. Has a universal search bar at the top — type any tyre size, claim ID, material name, or dealer name to filter every table at once.

Search bar

The search applies instantly to all tables. Examples:

  • Type 195/85 to find all rows for that tyre size across production, stock, and scrap
  • Type TFT to find all TFT (Thailand) procurement threads
  • Type a dealer name to find their invoices in the receivables table
  • Type overdue to show only overdue items

Production section

Shows PCR/Radial and Motorcycle production for the current year, broken down by tyre size. Each line shows: size, quantity produced, target (if set), acceptance rate, and reject rate.

Scrap Pareto The Scrap Pareto table is sorted by reject rate (not total count) so the worst quality sizes appear first. Sizes with fewer than 100 tyres produced are excluded (too small a sample to be meaningful).

Quality / OEE section

Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) = Availability × Performance × Quality.

MetricFormulaWhat it means
Availability (A)(Planned time − Downtime) ÷ Planned timeHow much time the machines were actually running vs scheduled
Performance (P)Actual output ÷ Ideal outputHow fast the line ran vs its rated speed
Quality (Q)Good tyres ÷ Total tyresFirst-pass yield — accepted vs all produced

If the downtime log is empty, the portal shows P×Q only (partial OEE) with a note "Log downtime in Capture → full OEE". Once captures include downtime records, full OEE appears automatically.

Orders & receivables section

Pulled from Galaxy iStock exports. Shows:

  • Outstanding balance — total money owed by all dealers
  • Overdue 60+ days — highest-risk receivables
  • Dealer credit table — sorted by balance, with aging bucket colored
Action guide 90+ days = pause credit + payment plan. 61–90 days = follow-up call. 31–60 days = send reminder.

CAPA & open actions

Shows all open corrective actions from captures — whiteboard photos, Viber extractions, and typed entries. Each action shows: description, owner, due date (color-coded), and status. Resolved actions are hidden.

Whiteboard captures

Shows all captured whiteboard records not yet classified as actions — raw data from photos and Viber messages, with date, source, and extracted values.

Capture & Entry screen (entry.html)

This is how the team records information — whiteboard photos, Viber messages, and typed notes. Everything captured here feeds into the CAPA board and the Full Detail screen automatically after the next refresh.

Photo capture (whiteboard OCR)

  1. Tap "Take photo" or the camera icon. On mobile this opens your camera directly.
  2. Point at the whiteboard clearly — make sure text is readable and not glare-blurred.
  3. Tap "Capture". The photo downloads to your device.
  4. Drop or upload the photo into the data/captures/ folder in Google Drive (or forward to admin).
  5. On the next scheduled refresh (or when admin runs sync), the photo is sent to AI vision and values extracted — output, reject count, downtime, action items.
Tip for better OCR Stand 1–1.5m from the board. Use natural light (not harsh flash). Write numbers large. Avoid overlapping text near the edges.

Viber paste (group messages)

  1. Open Viber Desktop on the office computer.
  2. Select the messages you want to record (e.g. order confirmations, stock movements, payments from the group chat).
  3. Copy (Ctrl+C).
  4. In the portal, tap "+ Capture" → Viber paste tab.
  5. Select the group name from the dropdown, then paste the text.
  6. Tap Save. The messages are queued for AI extraction on next refresh.

Supported record types: orders, payments, stock movements, delivery confirmations, claims, complaints.

Typed entry

For quick one-line records — abnormalities, maintenance notes, quality holds. Select the type from the dropdown, fill in the fields (description, area/machine, owner, due date), and tap Save.

Viewing saved captures

Tap List to see recent saved captures. Captures show as cards with their type, source, and extracted data. Records that have been promoted to the CAPA board show "Action created".

Notice Board (team collaboration)

The Notice Board is a shared space for the whole YTF team — announcements, shift handovers, and important reminders visible to all roles with permission. It is accessible from the home screen via the "Notices" button.

Posting a notice

  1. Tap "+ New notice" from the home screen or notice board.
  2. Select the group — which plant / head office this is for.
  3. Select the type — Announcement, Shift handover, Urgent alert, or General.
  4. Write your message. Keep it short and clear. You can mention an action owner with @name.
  5. Tap Post. The notice appears immediately to all users with access to that group.

Shift handover notices

At the end of each shift, the plant manager or floor manager should post a Shift handover notice. This tells the next shift what happened, what's pending, and any issues to watch. Standard fields:

  • Output this shift (e.g. PCR 240 pcs, MC 180 pcs)
  • Reject count + cause if high
  • Machine issues / downtime
  • Pending work from outgoing shift
  • Priority for incoming shift
Best practice Post handover notices before leaving the shift — not the next morning. The incoming shift reads them on their phone before they arrive.

Admin Console (admin.html)

Admin-only screen. Access via ops.supermega.dev/admin.html. Used to manage users, generate configuration, and monitor pipeline health.

Admin only Do not share the admin page URL with plant managers or regular managers. They should only access the main portal at ops.supermega.dev.

User management

The admin console generates the PANEL_USERS configuration that controls all access tokens and role assignments. To add or change a user:

  1. Open admin.html and log in with your admin token.
  2. In the Users section, add a row: role, display name, and Viber groups.
  3. The console generates a token for that user.
  4. Copy the generated PANEL_USERS JSON and paste it into Vercel → Settings → Environment Variables → PANEL_USERS.
  5. Trigger a redeploy (or it takes effect on the next deploy).
  6. Send the user their token (passcode). They enter it on the login screen.

Pipeline health

The admin console shows which data sources were last refreshed and when. Columns: source name, last refresh time, row count, and status. If a source shows "stale" (older than 48h), it means the scheduled refresh failed or the source file changed.

Running a manual refresh

  1. On the admin PC (with Node.js and the repo checked out), open PowerShell.
  2. Run ./sync.ps1 from the supermega-remote folder.
  3. The script pulls from Google Drive, rebuilds all data modules in parallel, and deploys to Vercel.
  4. Refresh your portal browser tab — new data appears immediately.

Module: Stock & Inventory

Raw material stock levels, updated from Drive workbooks and Galaxy iStock exports. Shows on-hand quantities, in-transit (expected), and days-to-stockout.

Days-to-stockout calculation

The portal estimates stockout date using average daily consumption from the last 30 days versus current on-hand + in-transit. This is an estimate — actual consumption varies by production plan.

In-transit materials

Materials ordered but not yet received. Sourced from TFT/KIIC email threads (CEO only) and Viber confirmations. Shows expected arrival date if known.

Stock by site

When multiple sites/warehouses are configured, stock shows per-site totals (Factory A / Factory B / Showroom / Head Office) plus consolidated.

Module: Quality / OEE / WCM

OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)

The three components are sourced differently:

ComponentData sourceStatus
Availability (A)Downtime captures (whiteboard, Viber, or typed entry)Active when captures logged
Performance (P)Actual output vs rated capacity from production workbookAlways active
Quality (Q)Accepted vs total from production workbookAlways active
To activate full OEE Log downtime events via Capture (entry.html). Each downtime record should include: machine/press, duration in minutes, and cause. Once any downtime capture exists, full A×P×Q is calculated and displayed automatically.

WCM / ISO scorecard

When quality_wcm: true is set in config, the portal shows a scorecard mapped to ISO 9001 / IATF 16949 pillars: Safety, Quality, Delivery, Cost, Morale (SQDCM). Enable this once WCM data is being captured regularly.

Closing an action

When an action is completed:

  1. In the ERP (ytf.supermega.dev), open the Work Board.
  2. Find the action, add proof (photo or note), and set status to "Resolved".
  3. After the next refresh, the action disappears from the CAPA board in the cockpit.

CEO / Owner daily workflow

Target time: 5 minutes every morning
  1. Open the portal on your phone (home screen icon → logs in automatically with your token).
  2. Check the header dot. Green = all normal. Yellow/Red = read the alerts below.
  3. Read the alerts in order. Top alert is the most critical. Each has a one-line action.
  4. Check production tile — are we on track for the month? If tyres YTD is behind, check with the plant manager.
  5. Check stock tile — any red (stock-out risk)? If yes, contact TFT or KIIC immediately.
  6. Tap "Full detail" if you need to drill into receivables (which dealers owe money), scrap by size, or claims.
  7. Check receivables — any 90+ day accounts? If yes, pause their credit and call for payment plan.
  8. Post a notice if you have a company-wide announcement or priority for the day.

Weekly review (Mondays)

  • Review the Scrap Pareto — are the same sizes consistently high-reject?
  • Review open CAPA items — any overdue more than 2 weeks?
  • Review receivables aging — any dealers worsening?
  • Check the trends tab — is production volume trending up or down?

Manager (Head Office) daily workflow

  1. Morning (8:00–8:15): Open portal, read alerts. Check stock levels for both plants. Any stock-out risks go to procurement immediately.
  2. Check notices: Read any shift handover notices from last night's plant managers.
  3. Production review: Open Full Detail → Production. Check yesterday's MTD vs plan. If behind, call plant manager.
  4. Orders check: Check receivables section — any new 60+ day accounts? Schedule follow-up.
  5. Mid-day: If any quality issues were flagged (scrap, claims), check CAPA board for owner and due date.
  6. End of day: Post a summary notice for both plants — what happened today, what's the priority for tomorrow.

Plant Manager daily workflow

  1. Start of shift (7:00): Read yesterday's handover notice in the Notice Board.
  2. Check your plant's production tile — are we on track for today's plan?
  3. Walk the line: Take whiteboard photo at end of morning shift. Open portal → Capture → Take photo → upload to Drive.
  4. Log any downtime: If a press/machine was stopped, open Capture → Typed entry → Downtime. Fill: machine, minutes, cause.
  5. Log quality issues: If reject count is high, use Capture → 5W1H to record Who / What / When / Where / Why / How.
  6. End of shift handover: Post a Shift Handover notice — output, rejects, machine issues, pending work, priorities for next shift.

Admin daily workflow

  1. Verify last refresh: Open admin.html → check "last refresh" timestamp. Should be within 6 hours. If stale, run sync.ps1.
  2. Check pipeline health: Any sources showing error or 0 rows? Common issue: Drive file renamed or moved.
  3. Review capture queue: Any whiteboard photos or Viber texts waiting for OCR? If so, run sync.ps1 with ANTHROPIC_API_KEY set.
  4. User requests: New staff joining? Generate their token in admin.html → update PANEL_USERS in Vercel.
  5. Galaxy export: Check if the iStock report has been exported and dropped in Drive (ideally weekly or after each sales period).

How often does it run?

Two modes:

  • Manual: Admin runs sync.ps1 on the office PC. Pulls fresh data, rebuilds all stages, deploys to Vercel. Takes ~3–5 minutes.
  • Automatic (GitHub Action): If configured, runs on a schedule (e.g. 6 AM + 6 PM daily). Requires the service account key and Anthropic key as GitHub secrets.

Setting up users

User access is controlled by the PANEL_USERS environment variable in Vercel. This is a JSON object where each key is a token (passcode) and the value contains the role and group assignments.

PANEL_USERS format

{
  "tok_abc123": { "role": "ceo",           "groups": ["*"],          "name": "U Ko Ko" },
  "tok_def456": { "role": "manager",       "groups": ["plant-a","plant-b","head-office"], "name": "Mg Mg" },
  "tok_ghi789": { "role": "plant_manager", "groups": ["plant-a"],    "name": "Ko Aung" },
  "tok_jkl012": { "role": "plant_manager", "groups": ["plant-b"],    "name": "Ma Hla" }
}

Adding a new user (step by step)

  1. Open admin.html → Users section.
  2. Add the user's role, name, and groups. The console generates a secure token.
  3. Copy the updated PANEL_USERS JSON.
  4. Go to vercel.com → your project (supermega-remote) → Settings → Environment Variables.
  5. Update or add PANEL_USERS with the new JSON value.
  6. Redeploy (Settings → Deployments → Redeploy latest).
  7. Send the new user their token — they enter it on the login screen.

Removing a user

Delete their token from PANEL_USERS → update in Vercel → redeploy. Their passcode immediately stops working.

Changing a role

Edit the "role" field in their token's entry in PANEL_USERS → update Vercel → redeploy. The user logs in with the same passcode but now sees a different data scope.

Connecting Galaxy iStock

Galaxy iStock has no public API — it runs on a local Windows server with a SQL Server database. There are three ways to get data out:

Option 1: Manual export (recommended to start)

  1. In Galaxy iStock: run Sales Register report → Export to Excel.
  2. Run Outstanding Receivables report → Export to Excel.
  3. Drop both files into the shared Google Drive folder called "Galaxy export".
  4. Run sync.ps1 — the adapter finds the files automatically by name pattern.
  5. Portal shows updated orders and receivables.

Recommended frequency: weekly, or after each major sales period.

Option 2: Scheduled SQL export (automatic)

If you can give admin a read-only login to the Galaxy SQL Server, a connector script (galaxy-sql.mjs) can pull data automatically on each refresh — no manual export needed. Contact admin to set this up. What you need:

  • The SQL Server hostname or IP (accessible on LAN or via VPN)
  • A read-only SQL login (not the Galaxy admin password)
  • The Galaxy database name

Option 3: ODBC view (durable)

A database administrator creates read-only SQL views over the sales, receivables, and stock tables, then exposes them via ODBC. Most stable long-term — contact the Galaxy support or your IT team to set up.

Viber integration

How it works today

The team copies Viber messages from the group chat and pastes them into the portal's Capture screen. The AI extracts structured data (orders, payments, deliveries, stock info) from the pasted text and adds it to the relevant module.

Paste workflow (manual, available now)

  1. Open Viber Desktop on the office PC.
  2. Select the messages you want to record from the group chat.
  3. Copy (Ctrl+C).
  4. Open the portal → Capture → Viber paste tab.
  5. Select the group from the dropdown.
  6. Paste the text.
  7. Tap Save. Messages are processed on the next refresh.

Viber bot webhook (automatic — production path)

For fully automatic Viber integration, a Viber bot can be set up to receive group messages and send them to the portal automatically. This requires:

  • A Viber bot auth token from the Viber developer console
  • The token stored as YTF_VIBER_AUTH_TOKEN in Vercel
  • The portal deployed with the webhook endpoint active

Contact admin to set this up. Once configured, Viber messages are processed automatically without any manual copying.

Glossary

TermMeaning
OEEOverall Equipment Effectiveness — A × P × Q. Industry standard for measuring manufacturing productivity.
CAPACorrective Action and Preventive Action. A formal process for identifying root cause of defects and preventing recurrence.
PCRPassenger Car Radial — the radial tyre production line at Plant A.
MCMotorcycle — the motorcycle tyre production line.
TFTThai company (Thailand) — the main raw material supplier for rubber compounds and polyester cord.
KIICKorea-based material supplier for steel cord and other compounds.
YTDYear to date — total from January 1 to today.
MTDMonth to date — total from the 1st of this month to today.
5W1HWho, What, When, Where, Why, How — a structured format for recording abnormalities and incidents.
WCMWorld Class Manufacturing — a methodology for operational excellence. YTF maps its quality metrics to WCM pillars.
IATF 16949International quality management standard for automotive production.
Galaxy iStockThe inventory and accounting software used at YTF showrooms and head office.
PWAProgressive Web App — the portal installs on your phone home screen and works like a native app.
PANEL_USERSThe Vercel environment variable that stores all user tokens, roles, and group assignments.
Drive service accountA Google service account (a robot Google account) that the portal uses to read Drive files automatically, without anyone being logged in.
Viber bot webhookA system where Viber sends group messages to the portal automatically when the bot is added to the group.
Aging bucketA category for how old a receivable is: 0–30 / 31–60 / 61–90 / 90+ days.
Scrap ParetoA ranking of tyre sizes by their reject rate, used to find the worst quality issues first.
Days-to-stockoutEstimated number of days until a raw material runs out, based on current stock and average daily consumption.
Insight / SignalAn automatically generated alert based on cross-module analysis (e.g. stock-out risk detected by comparing inventory vs consumption).
